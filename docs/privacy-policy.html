NapStop Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026

NapStop ("the app") is developed and operated by Robert Bartis ("I", "me", or "my"). This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and the choices you have.

1. Information Collected

Location Data

NapStop's core function is location-based alarms. To deliver this, the app requests Always On location access so it can monitor your proximity to saved alarm locations even when the app is in the background or the screen is off.

  • Location data is processed entirely on your device.
  • Your location is never transmitted to any server operated by me.
  • Location is not stored beyond what is needed to trigger an alarm you have set.

Crash and Diagnostic Data

The app uses Firebase Crashlytics (provided by Google) to automatically collect crash reports and basic diagnostic information when the app encounters an error. This data includes:

  • Device model and iOS version
  • App version and build number
  • Stack traces at the time of a crash

This information is used solely to identify and fix software defects. It does not include your name, email, or precise location. Google's privacy practices are described at policies.google.com/privacy.

Data You Enter

Alarm names, locations, radii, and settings you configure are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework, optionally synced to your personal iCloud account. This data never passes through servers I operate.

2. Data Not Collected

NapStop does not collect, store, or share:

  • Your name, email address, or any account credentials
  • Contacts, photos, microphone, or camera data
  • Advertising identifiers or tracking data
  • Precise location history sent to any third party

There are no user accounts, no sign-in, and no advertising in this app.

3. Third-Party Services

The only third-party SDK integrated into NapStop is Firebase Crashlytics (Google). No analytics, advertising, or social SDKs are included.

GTFS transit schedule data (used for transit feed features) is downloaded directly from public agency sources and is not associated with your identity in any way.

4. Children's Privacy

NapStop is not directed at children under 13. I do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

5. Your Choices

  • Location access — you can change location permissions at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → NapStop. Restricting location access will prevent alarms from triggering in the background.
  • iCloud sync — you can disable iCloud for NapStop in Settings → [your name] → iCloud → NapStop. Alarm data will remain local only.
  • Crash reporting — crash reports are linked to an anonymous installation ID generated by Firebase. There is no way to associate them with you personally.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

All alarm data stored on your device is deleted when you delete the app. Crash reports retained by Firebase are subject to Google's standard retention policies.
